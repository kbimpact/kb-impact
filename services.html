What We Do

Services

Focused engagements that build the development strategy and the data infrastructure underneath it. Every engagement is scoped with named deliverables and a flat fee. You know exactly what will be produced before I start.

KB Consulting works with nonprofits across Sheboygan County, Wisconsin, and the Midwest.

Core Engagements

Project engagements

Time-bound, flat-fee projects with a defined scope and written deliverables. Strategy without data infrastructure produces plans that cannot be executed. Data work without strategy produces clean reports no one acts on. These engagements integrate both. The Development Assessment is the recommended starting point for most organizations.

Development Assessment

$5,000-$6,000  ||  4-6 weeks

A written review of your current development function: revenue sources, donor trends, staffing and systems capacity, and infrastructure gaps. Produces a donor database audit, RFM scoring framework, donor segmentation map (Champions, Loyal, Potential, At-Risk, Low Priority), and a prioritized recommendation set with sequenced next steps.

Development Strategy

$10,000-$12,000  ||  6-12 weeks

The comprehensive planning engagement. Produces: annual fundraising calendar, gift table, major gifts strategy, non-government grant strategy and prospect framework (advisory only), case for support framework, donor journey map, stewardship matrix, and prospect prioritization list with cultivation notes. Can follow a Development Assessment or stand alone for organizations with existing baseline data.

Data Infrastructure

$7,000-$9,000  ||  6-12 weeks

Implementation of donor data systems that work. CRM audit and configuration, data hygiene and migration, donor segmentation built inside the system rather than rebuilt in spreadsheets, RFM scoring logic, reporting and dashboard architecture, and staff training. Primary platform fluency in Neon CRM and Salesforce, adaptable to other systems. Appropriate for organizations transitioning between platforms, recovering from a stalled implementation, or operating a system that produces reports staff cannot trust.

Add-On Engagements

Focused add-ons

Standalone or companion engagements that address a specific gap. Can run independently or alongside a project engagement.

Board Activation

$3,000-$4,500

Board engagement assessment, resource development committee formation support, board giving framework, role clarity in fundraising (cultivation vs. stewardship vs. solicitation), and a facilitated board session. Addresses the gap between board members who show up for the gala and board members who are active in donor relationships.

Campaign Development

$4,000-$6,000

Pre-campaign advisory only, not campaign management or execution. Produces: pre-campaign feasibility assessment, case for support, campaign brief narrative, gift table, ask sequencing plan, and major donor prospect identification and prioritization. Campaign execution is scoped separately.

Fundraising Event Support

Scoped per engagement

Strategic advisory and coordination for organizations planning a fundraising event. Scope is defined per engagement based on where the most help is needed. Coverage can include event strategy and planning framework, event story framework, committee coordination, sponsorship strategy and prospect development, vendor coordination, and a post-event engagement plan. The post-event engagement piece is where most organizations lose the donors they acquire at the event, which is where this engagement does its highest-value work.

Entry Points

Discrete project work

For organizations not yet ready for a full engagement. These are trust-building entry points, not substitutes for structured work.

Focused Strategic Memo

$1,000

A written analysis of a specific, bounded question: a funding decision, a donor situation, a board structure problem. Scoped to a single issue. Delivered as a written memo with findings and recommendations.

Working Document

$3,500-$6,000

A single scoped deliverable outside a full engagement: a case for support, a stewardship matrix, a gift table, a CRM audit, or a comparable standalone document. Price reflects scope and complexity.

Donor-Facing Narrative

Storytelling runs through every engagement.

Data tells you who to ask and for how much. Storytelling is how the ask lands. Case for support, donor journey maps, campaign brief narratives, and event story frameworks are built into the relevant engagements rather than scoped separately.

Know what you are getting before we start.

Every engagement begins with a defined scope and a Scope of Services document. Deliverables are named upfront. Scope changes are documented as amendments. The conversation about what you need comes first.
